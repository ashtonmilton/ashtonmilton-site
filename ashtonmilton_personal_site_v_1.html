Research

My research focuses on the political economy of development, institutional transformation, and public policy under conditions of accelerated economic growth. I am particularly interested in how states develop unevenly across policy domains, especially where investment-facing institutions strengthen faster than labour, welfare, or redistributive systems.

Current Dissertation

Selective State Capacity under Accelerated Development

Investment prioritisation, labour informalisation, and transitional inequality in Indonesia.

Indonesia Labour Governance Political Economy

Project Overview

This project examines how development states may strengthen investment-facing institutions while leaving labour, welfare, and redistributive institutions comparatively weak. Using Indonesia as a case study, the dissertation explores the relationship between investment prioritisation, labour informalisation, and transitional inequality.


The project is organised around the concept of selective state capacity: the uneven development of state capability across different institutional and policy domains. Rather than treating state capacity as a single national attribute, the project asks why some capacities develop earlier, faster, or more coherently than others.

Research Question

How does accelerated development produce uneven state capacity across investment, labour, welfare, and redistributive institutions?

Working Argument

Investment-oriented governance may be strengthened because it directly supports growth, competitiveness, and state legitimacy, while redistributive institutions remain politically and administratively harder to consolidate.

Methodological Direction

The project uses qualitative policy analysis, institutional analysis, and a case-study approach, supported by secondary data and policy literature.

Research Interests

These interests are deliberately connected. Together, they form a research trajectory around governance, development, inequality, and institutional capacity in East and Southeast Asia.

State Capacity & Institutional Development

I am interested in how states build institutional capacity unevenly across sectors. My current work examines how governments may develop strong investment and infrastructure capabilities while labour protections, welfare systems, and redistributive institutions remain weaker.

Development Governance

This area focuses on how governments manage economic transformation under conditions of rapid growth, industrialisation, and global integration. I am especially interested in governance trade-offs, institutional sequencing, and the social consequences of development strategy.

Political Economy of Southeast Asia

Southeast Asia is a core regional focus because it offers cases of accelerated but uneven development. Indonesia is my primary case study, with wider interests in ASEAN, middle-income development, and Global South comparison.

Labour, Informality & Inequality

A recurring focus of my work is the persistence of informal labour and uneven social protection in high-growth economies. I am interested in how labour systems evolve under investment-led development models and how these patterns shape long-term inequality.

Comparative Public Policy

I examine how policy frameworks differ across institutional and political contexts, especially in relation to development strategy, welfare provision, governance effectiveness, and state coordination capacity.

East Asian Urban & Institutional Systems

Living and studying in Japan has strengthened my interest in East Asian urban governance, public infrastructure, institutional coordination, and social organisation. I am interested in how urban systems reflect wider governance capacity.

Global Governance & International Development

I am interested in how international organisations, regional institutions, and multilateral development frameworks interact with domestic development priorities, particularly across Asia and the wider Global South.

China, Shanghai & Policy Learning

As a prospective postgraduate direction, I am interested in China as a site for studying governance, public policy, development strategy, and state-led institutional coordination from within an Asian policy environment.
